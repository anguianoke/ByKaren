TARGET CIRCLE

Target Circle is Target's free loyalty program designed to go beyond the benefits of in-store shopping by offering exclusive deals, rewards, and tailored offers that cater to each user's preferences. Ultimately, their goal is to ensure every Target shopping spree is a rewarding and convenient interaction.

Project Overview

Role: I was tasked to deliver an experience that is both functional and fulfilling. I conducted research to understand the common expectations, wants, and needs of a user on a prominent shopping platform.

Goal: My goal for this project is to redesign the Target Circle App in order to provide a more personalized, intuitive, and satisfying experience for both new and experienced users.

Approach: Bottom-Up — Preserve Target's iconic aesthetic as much as possible, while implementing thoughtful adjustments to enhance navigation and user engagement.

DISCOVER & DEFINE

Exploratory User Research

Research Goals

  • Assess the pain points and frustrations that users experience when navigating the Target Circle app.
  • Gain insights into the needs and goals of both new and experienced users.
  • Identify the common user expectations and industry standards for shopping apps.
  • Evaluate the effectiveness of the current app's personalization factor.

User Needs

Users of the Target Circle app (regardless of their experience level) need an app that:

  • Is personalized to their needs and shopping habits.
  • Is easy to navigate with a clear and uncluttered design.
  • Has a functional search and filter function.
  • Is engaging, with features that provide value and encourage repeat use.

Goals

What users expect to get out of this app:

  • Save money on purchases: Users expect the app to help them find the best deals and discounts available.
  • Conveniently find and manage their products: Users seek an app that simplifies the process of finding and managing the products they want. They desire features like wishlists, previous order history, and the ability to easily search and filter products.

Shared Pain Points

  • Navigation Challenges
    • Hidden features: Features hidden from view or poorly communicated to users restrict them from discovering valuable information that could have increased engagement.
    • Confusing filtering and categorization: Filtering options are unclear or disorganized, making it hard for users to find what they are looking for.
  • Poor Streamlined Design
    • Cluttered layout and information overload: Users consistently mentioned feeling frustrated and overwhelmed by the amount of information they were running into.
    • Lack of personalized content: Users expressed a desire for a more personalized experience, where deals and recommendations align with their shopping habits. Generic and irrelevant information reduced the app's relevance to them.
    • Limited customization options: The app provided limiting opportunities for users to customize their experience and tailor the app to their needs.

Problem

  • Hidden Features
  • Confusing Filtering and Categorization

Solution

Simplify Navigation and Filters: Redesign the app's menus, layout order, and filtering options to be more intuitive.

  • Group related items together
  • Use clear labels and visual cues
  • Organize to fit industry standards

Reduce cognitive overload, making it easier for users to find what they are looking for and encouraging them to explore the app further.

Solution

Streamline Design: Create a visually appealing and less cluttered interface.

A more inviting experience that enhances readability and reduces the sense of overwhelm for users. Additionally, have increased user engagement by fostering a more social and enjoyable atmosphere within the app.

Reflection

My research presented 3 primary user types:

  • Experienced Users - Individuals who have been shopping at Target and are familiar with the app.
  • Inexperienced Users - Users who are either new to the app or infrequent Target shoppers.
  • Tech-Savvy Users - While they may be new to Target and the app, they are familiar with similar interfaces and have vast experience with e-commerce platforms.

The tech-savvy user came in handy because they were able to provide me a general idea of what other apps are doing to keep their users engaged and happy. Seeing how they and the inexperienced users navigated the app helped me determine where they expected certain features to be, what seemed out of place, and what actually did work for them.

With the experienced users, it was interesting to see that there were still some things that they had not been aware existed within the app, indicating that the interface contained hidden features that could be revised to appear more noticeable or easier to access.

Despite the vast areas of expertise, all users shared common goals and pain points, which sparked the idea for this prototype.

Challenges I Faced

Figuring out what to do with and how to organize all the information without getting overwhelmed myself was a bit of a task. I didn’t want to take something that was clearly visible in the original design and hide it just for the sake of making something else more visible. I think that implementing a more accurate algorithm will help with the cluttering and prioritize mainly what the user wants to see first.
